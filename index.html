What Makes John....Buddha.

I have my heart set on becoming a Software Engineer. I began coding in 2001, my senior year in highschool when I discovered Computer Science as an elective. While taking the class, one of the teachers aids was doing some web designing and I was intersesting in learning it. That was my first encounter with HTML. I didn't have an opportunity to continue learning after graduation as I did not go to college right after. When Starbucks launched the SCAP, Starbucks College Achievement Plan, I saw an opportunity to get a college degree. What really got me excited was when I saw Software Engineering on the list of available degrees to pursue.

Family Matters

One of the main reasons I am pursuing a degree in Software Engineering is because of my family.

Over the years I have just worked to provide enough for my family. Now I want to get a career, not only to provide more for my family, but to do something that I love. That is working with computers in many aspects like programming and building PCs. The past 2 years, my oldest son has shown an interest in what I have been going to school. He says now he wants to make videos games. I am really excited to help him along the way if that is something he really wants to do in the future. My wife, Mariah, and I have been married since December, 6, 2014. It has been wonderful and part of the reason I am back in school is because of her push and support, also Starbucks paying for it. I have been with Starbucks for 15 years now and hopefully when I graduate, I can stay with Starbucks and work in there tech area with my degree.
